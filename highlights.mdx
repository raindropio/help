Reading online means scanning dozens of pages a day — and losing the important parts just as fast.
Select and save key passages from any web page, PDF, or EPUB to revisit them later without re-reading the whole page.

Color-code your highlights, add annotations, then search or export them across all your bookmarks.

<Note>Highlights are free for all users with no limit on pages or highlights. [Annotations](#adding-a-note) (notes on highlights) require a [Pro plan](/premium-features).</Note>

## Saving highlights

Select any text and pick a color — the highlight is saved instantly. You can also add an optional note. See how it works on each platform below.

            <Step>

                <Frame>![](/assets/highlights-manage.png)</Frame>

            <Step>
                Pick a highlight color from the toolbar that appears, and optionally add an annotation.
            </Step>
        </Steps>
    </Tab>

    <Tab title="Browser extension">
        <Steps>
            <Step>
                Select text on any web page — click and drag over the passage you want to save.

                <Frame>![](/assets/highlights-extension.png)</Frame>
            </Step>
            <Step>
                Choose **Save highlight** from the context menu, or use a keyboard shortcut:

                | Browser | Shortcut |
                |---------|----------|
                | Chrome, Safari | `Ctrl+Shift+S` (Mac: `Cmd+Shift+S`) |
                | Edge, Firefox, others | `Alt+X` (Mac: `⌃X`) |

                <Warning>In Safari on macOS, click **Always Allow on Every Website** when prompted — otherwise highlights won't appear on revisits.</Warning>
            </Step>
        </Steps>
    </Tab>

## Annotating

Leave a note on any highlight — useful for capturing your thoughts in context.
Tap or click a highlight, then tap the note icon to write your note.

<Note>Annotations require a [Pro plan](/premium-features). Highlights themselves are free.</Note>

## Editing highlights

Tap or click any saved highlight to open an action toolbar (available on all platforms):

- **Change color** — switch between available highlight colors
- **Add note** — attach an annotation to the highlight
- **Delete** — remove the highlight from the page

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
    <Accordion title="Can I highlight PDFs and EPUBs?">
        Yes. Save a PDF as a bookmark or [upload it as a file](/files), then open it inside Raindrop.io to highlight. EPUBs work the same way — upload to any collection and open. The browser's built-in PDF viewer doesn't support Raindrop.io highlights.
    </Accordion>

    <Accordion title="Why does the browser extension need extra permissions?">
        To display your saved highlights when you revisit a page, the extension needs **Access data on websites**. It checks the page URL and loads matching highlights — no tracking or third-party data sharing involved. Many extensions request this permission by default; Raindrop.io asks explicitly.
    </Accordion>

    <Accordion title="Are highlights included in backups?">
        Yes. Highlights are part of your [backup](/export) data alongside collections, bookmarks, and tags.
    </Accordion>

    <Accordion title="Do highlights sync across devices?">
        Yes. Highlights are stored on Raindrop.io servers and appear everywhere you're signed in.
    </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
